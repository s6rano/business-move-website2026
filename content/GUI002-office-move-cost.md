# How much does an office move cost in Belgium? Benchmarks to guide you

**You need to put a figure on your office move, and you quickly hit the same wall: from one provider to the next, the amounts quoted vary by a factor of two. In those conditions it is hard to tell whether a quote is reasonable — or to defend a budget internally.**

That is normal: a business move has no catalogue price. It depends on a few concrete factors, which this article sets out, with ranges to place yourself and a method to get a reliable figure for your case.

> There is no single price, only a range you can frame.

## Why there is no single price

Two companies of the same size can pay two or three times as much as each other. The cost mainly depends on five variables:

- the volume to move (furniture, workstations, archives, stock);
- the distance between the old and the new site;
- access conditions: floors, lift, corridor width, parking for the lorry;
- the IT equipment to unplug, transport and put back into service;
- the timing: an evening, weekend or public-holiday move costs more.

A serious quote always starts from these elements. A price given without a visit or an inventory therefore means little.

## The main cost items

To read a quote, it helps to know what it contains. An office move usually breaks down as follows:

- labour: the moving crew, often the largest item;
- transport: lorries, fuel, the number of trips depending on distance;
- packing and materials: boxes, protection, IT crates;
- difficult access: a furniture hoist (the external lift that takes loads up the façade) is charged separately;
- IT: disconnection, secure transport, reconnection;
- insurance for furniture and equipment in transit;
- out-of-hours surcharges: evening, weekend, public holiday.

These items do not all appear in a "lowest-price" quote. That is often where the gap between two offers opens up.

## Ranges to place yourself

Here are orders of magnitude, to handle with care: they depend entirely on the factors above. They are there to place you, not to replace a quote.

- A small office (a few workstations): often €1,500 to €5,000.
- A floor or an SME (twenty to fifty workstations): often €5,000 to €20,000.
- A head office or large organisation (beyond that): €20,000 to over €100,000.

Another common way to reason is to estimate a cost per workstation. It is useful for a first approach, but access, IT and weekend work can move that figure sharply.

> These amounts are indicative benchmarks, to be confirmed by a quote after a technical visit.

## The costs people forget

Transport is only part of the real bill. Several expenses often slip under the radar when the budget is drawn up:

- reinstating the old site (painting, repairs, removing fit-out), often required by the lease;
- decommissioning: dismantling and clearing out whatever does not follow;
- new or adapted furniture for the new premises;
- unforeseen costs on the day (blocked access, extra equipment, extra hours).

Anticipating them avoids nasty surprises. We cover this in a dedicated article (see "Read also").

## The invisible cost: internal time

One item appears on no quote: the hours your team spends preparing, coordinating and following the project. Someone has to frame the need, compare the offers, be present on the day. That time has a value, especially when it is added to an already busy role.

It is a decision criterion in itself: handling it in-house costs less in direct euros but takes up time; delegating costs more but frees up your teams.

## How to get a reliable figure for your case

Ranges place you; they do not replace an estimate. To get close to your actual cost:

1. make a clear inventory of what moves, goes to storage or is thrown out;
2. use our calculator to get an indicative estimate in a few minutes;
3. ask for three quotes on the same assumptions (same volume, same date, same services), so that they are comparable.

If you would rather not steer all this yourself, a specialist agency can take on the framing, the comparison and the coordination — an option to weigh against the internal time mentioned above.

## In short

An office move has no single price: it is framed. Use the ranges to place yourself, the calculator for a first estimate, and three comparable quotes to decide with confidence.
